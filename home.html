Introduction

I am a Research Scientist at the Shenzhen Research Institute of Big Data and an Adjunct Assistant Professor in the School of Data Science at The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen. I received my Ph.D. in Electronic and Computer Engineering from the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST) in 2022, and my B.Eng. in Communication Engineering from Southeast University in 2017 (Chien‑Shiung Wu Honor College, Advanced Class). My research spans sparse/efficient AI, physically grounded world model, and AI for mathematical optimization.

Research

My group builds Field World Models (FWM): physically grounded, data-driven models of real-world fields that fuse geometry, materials, and multimodal measurements. We aim to deliver environment-aware communication and decision-making that are interpretable, editable, and deployable. While our current focus is electromagnetics/wireless, the same methodology naturally extends to turbulence, acoustics, optics, and thermal domains.

Our work spans three tightly coupled thrusts:

  • Field World Model (FWM): Learn spatial fields and dynamics from multimodal data (e.g., BIM/point clouds/maps, vision/language, IMU/GNSS, RF sweeps/arrays). We combine neural and physics-based components (NeRF/3DGS, diffusion/energy models, GNNs, neural operators such as FNO/DeepONet/GNO) with PDE and boundary consistency, and structured sparsity/low-rank priors. The models support editable inversion, uncertainty quantification, and active re-measurement.

  • Sparse and Efficient AI: Develop structured sparsity, low-rank, and tensorization methods, with unified compression/distillation across data, features, models, and gradients. We enable edge–cloud collaboration, federated training, and HW/SW co-acceleration (CPU/GPU/FPGA) for reliable on-device deployment.

  • Learning-based Optimization (L2O): Build differentiable and learned solvers (RL/GNN/neural combinatorial optimization) that operate on FWM to make constrained decisions. Targets include site placement, beam/power/spectrum allocation, routing/formation planning, with feasibility guarantees, learned warm-starts, and fast cross-scenario adaptation.

Introduction

I am currently a tenure-track associate professor at the School of Intelligent Systems Engineering, Sun Yat-sen University (SYSU). Before joining SYSU, I was a Research Scientist at the Shenzhen Research Institute of Big Data and an Adjunct Assistant Professor in the School of Data Science at The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen. I received my Ph.D. in Electronic and Computer Engineering from the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST), and my B.Eng. in Communication Engineering from Southeast University (Chien‑Shiung Wu Honor College, Advanced Class). My research spans sparse/efficient AI, physically grounded world model, and AI for mathematical optimization.

Research

FieldMind: a physics-grounded world model for physical fields (EM, flow/turbulence, acoustics, optics, thermal) and real-time decision making.

Our vision is Smart/Intelligent Physical Modeling and Decision Making—interpretable, editable, and deployable systems that understand the physical world and act in it safely and efficiently. Unlike mainstream visual world models centered on appearance and geometry, our focus is the governing physical fields that drive communication, transport, and control.

We organize our work as a closed loop around FieldMind:

  • Seeing the field world: Multimodal Physical Field Modeling (EM-first)
    We fuse geometry/materials (BIM/point clouds/maps), context (vision/language/IMU/GNSS), and field measurements (RF sweeps/arrays for EM; probes/sensors for flow, acoustics, thermal) into a unified FieldMind model. We use 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS), NeRF/variants, VGGT and cross-modal transformers as alignment/representation tools, coupled with PDE and boundary consistency, constitutive relations, and material priors. The model quantifies uncertainty and supports editable inversion, and naturally extends from EM/wireless to turbulence/fluids, acoustics, optics, and thermal domains.

  • Making it compact and complete: Sparse/Low-Dimensional Structures
    Multimodal field data are high-dimensional and often partially observed. We exploit structured sparsity, low-rank/tensorized representations, and sparse latent diffusion/energy models to achieve: (i) edge-efficient compression and fast field reconstruction with calibrated uncertainty; (ii) principled field completion in unsensed/occluded regions via physics-aware generative priors; (iii) reliability and certifiability for downstream use. The result is a lightweight, robust, and transferable field representation.

  • Turning understanding into action: Learning to Optimize with HW/SW Co-Design
    We tightly couple FieldMind with differentiable, learning-augmented optimizers for real-time, certifiable planning and control—multi-UAV/UAM coordination, spectrum/beam/power scheduling, and site–trajectory co-design under interference, airspace, and environmental constraints. Through algorithm–system co-design (C++/CUDA/FPGA, federated/edge execution), we map structural sparsity into solvers and hardware for end-to-end speed/energy gains and rapid replanning.
